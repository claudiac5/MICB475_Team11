# P07 - Identify the Specific Bacteria that Best Distinguish Intestinal vs Diffuse Cancers 

March 6, 2026

## Purpose:
To determine if specific bacterial genera act as biomarkers or are significantly differentially abundant between the Intestinal and Diffuse subtypes of Gastric Cancer (GC). Additionally, this aim investigates whether specific microbial drivers within the Diffuse subtypes are significantly modulated by biological sex.

## Results: 
Using both ANCOM-BC2 and Indicator Species Analysis, we identified 24 genera that significantly differed between the Intestinal and Diffuse gastric cancer subtypes. The volcano plot shows how these significant taxa are distributed based on their log fold change and q-values, highlighting genera that are enriched in one subtype compared to the other. The heatmap further supports these results, as hierarchical clustering grouped samples based on the relative abundance patterns of these 24 genera. Together, these visualizations suggest that specific microbial taxa are associated with each gastric cancer subtype.

## Discussion:
Identifying 24 significantly different genera suggests that the microbial community composition changes depending on the gastric cancer subtype. The clustering pattern seen in the heatmap indicates that these differences are consistent across samples rather than random variation. This supports the idea that the tumor microenvironment may influence, or be influenced by, specific microbial communities. In other words, certain microbial profiles may be associated with either the Intestinal or Diffuse subtype of gastric cancer.

## Future direction:
Investigate the biological roles of the 24 genera identified in this analysis. This could involve reviewing the literature to see whether these taxa have been previously linked to inflammation, immune responses, or cancer-related processes. In addition, these results could be used for further analyses, such as predictive modeling or functional inference using tools like PICRUSt2, which could help determine whether these microbial communities differ in their potential metabolic or functional capabilities.
