# P04 - QIIME 2 Alpha-Rarefaction

February 20, 2026

## Purpose:
To determine an appropriate sequencing depth for diversity analysis and assess whether sequencing depth captured the majority of microbial diversity.

## Material: 
- Filtered feature table
- Rooted phylogenetic tree
- Sample metadata

## Method:
1. A phylogenetic tree was generated from the representative sequences.
2. Alpha rarefaction analysis was performed to evaluate diversity across sequencing depths.
3. Rarefaction curves were examined to determine a sampling depth that balanced maximizing ASV detection while retaining most samples.
4. A sampling depth was selected based on the rarefaction curve plateau and sample retention.

## Results: 
The rarefaction curves approached a plateau for most samples, indicating that sequencing depth was sufficient to capture the majority of microbial diversity. A sampling depth of 8386 reads allowed retention of most samples while maintaining sufficient sequencing coverage.

## Discussion:
Selecting a sampling depth near the plateau allowed retention of most samples while capturing the majority of microbial diversity.

## Future direction:
Use the selected sampling depth to perform alpha and beta diversity analyses.
